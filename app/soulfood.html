SOULFOOD

Final degree project during my senior year at Northeastern University. The project explores and challenges the stigmas and stereotypes associated with mental illness, specifically anxiety and depression, through a series of printed zines that offer practical mindfulness exercises.

Inspired by a transformative mindfulness course I took in my final years at Northeastern and my own practice, I was interested in presenting the both spiritual and scientific nature of mindfulness in a way that defied the visual stereotypes of particular religions and the self-help genre. Despite the prevalence of stress, anxiety, and depression in our own lives and in the lives of those we care about, the stigmas associated with mental illness keep us from openly communicating about them or accepting them on the same level of physical illnesses.

My aim was to introduce mindfulness concepts in an unassuming, casual way. Through the informal physical medium reminiscent of note-passing amongst friends, each zine provides a catalyst for discussion, and unfolds into an 11x17 inch poster.
